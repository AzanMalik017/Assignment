Virtual Reality (VR) is a computer-generated simulation of an environment that can be interacted with in a seemingly real or physical way by a person using special electronic equipment, such as a VR headset.

VR works by presenting users with a three-dimensional, computer-generated environment that they can explore and interact with through specialized hardware, such as VR headsets and hand controllers. These devices track the user's movements and adjust the virtual environment accordingly, creating a sense of immersion and presence.

Future trends in VR include advancements in hardware technology, such as higher-resolution displays and improved motion tracking, expanded content creation and distribution, integration with other emerging technologies like augmented reality (AR) and artificial intelligence (AI), and increased adoption in various industries beyond gaming and entertainment.
